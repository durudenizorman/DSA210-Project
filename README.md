## 1. Project Motivation
Isotretinoin is highly effective for treating acne but is notoriously known for its severe side effects. The dosage used in the treatment plays a significant role in patient experience. As a student currently undergoing this treatment, I have experienced various side effects that fluctuated according to my dosage. 

My primary motivation for this project is to apply data science methodologies to determine if there is a statistical causation and/or correlation between daily dosage levels, the intensity (severity) of reported side effects, and overall patient sentiment. By analyzing public datasets, I aim to create a predictive model that helps manage symptoms beforehand.

## 2. Datasets (Data Enrichment)
To ensure a robust analysis, I utilized two distinct public datasets:

1. **FDA FAERS Database (`isotretinoin_project_data.csv`):** * **Source:** Extracted via Python API from the US Food and Drug Administration's Adverse Event Reporting System.
   * **Features:** Patient Age, Gender, Dosage (mg), and Reported Side Effects.
2. **Drugs.com Patient Reviews (`isotretinoin_reviews.csv`):** * **Source:** Public drug review repository.
   * **Features:** Raw review text, Rating (1-10), and Usefulness Count. 
   * *Note:* Dosage was extracted from raw text using NLP/Regex techniques.

## 3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
During the initial EDA phase (April 14 Deliverable), I focused on understanding the distribution of dosages and the characteristics of the patient population. 
* A custom `severity_score` was generated by counting the number of side effects reported per FDA case.
* Visualizations indicate a higher prevalence of younger users and predominantly 20mg, 40mg, and 60mg daily dosages.

## 4. Hypothesis Testing
Three formal hypotheses were tested to validate the project's core assumptions:

* **H1 (Dosage vs. Severity):** Tested the correlation between daily dosage and the number of reported side effects. 
  * *Result:* No strong linear correlation found (p > 0.05). Severity is likely influenced by biological factors rather than raw dosage alone.
* **H2 (Dosage vs. Sentiment):** Tested the relationship between extracted dosage and patient satisfaction ratings.
  * *Result:* No negative correlation found. Patients report high satisfaction despite high dosages.
* **H3 (Gender vs. Severity) [Key Finding]:** Conducted an Independent T-Test to compare side effect severity between genders.
  * *Result:* **Statistically Significant (p < 0.05).** Male patients report a significantly higher average number of side effects (3.34) compared to female patients (2.75).

## 5. Future Work & Machine Learning (May 5 Deliverable)
The discovery of Gender as a statistically significant feature shifts the focus of the predictive model. The future Machine Learning pipeline will aim to predict the expected number of side effects (severity) for a new patient based on their Age, Gender, and prescribed Dosage, directly addressing the project's initial goal of symptom management.
